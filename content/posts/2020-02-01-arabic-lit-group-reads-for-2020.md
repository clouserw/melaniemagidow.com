This year in the [MENA Lit Group](https://www.goodreads.com/group/show/413-middle-east-north-african-lit) on Goodreads.com we’re planning four group reads from Day 1, to be expanded if interest demands.

Here are our 4 seasons of reading for 2020:

![](/images/300de-seven.days_.of_.man_.png)

1. [Seven Days of Man](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/1277693.The_Seven_Days_of_Man?from_search=true&qid=cOHKYz5WSf&rank=2)  by Abdel Hakim Qasem(January-March) | Set in an Egyptian village in  northern Egypt in the Delta near Tanta, this classic novel tells a  coming-of-age tale of a young man trying to find his place in the world.

![](/images/51eb7-the.quarter.png)

2. [The Quarter](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/45428966-the-quarter?from_search=true&qid=11npqDD3ZC&rank=1)  by Naguib Mahfouz (April-June) | This manuscript by the late Nobel  laureate was discovered last year among his personal papers. With Roger  Allen’s translation in English now available, we’ll be among the first  to read this Mahfouz work!

![](/images/3ce74-an.apartment.called.freedom.png)

3. [An Apartment Called Freedom](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/1652330.An_Apartment_Called_Freedom?from_search=true&qid=snWXFMyHAc&rank=5)  by Ghazi Algosaibi (July-September) | This novel tells the story of  four young men from Bahrain living as students in Cairo in the late 50s  and early 60s (Nasser’s Egypt). Adapted into a popular MBC TV series in  1995.

![](/images/ec2e9-travels.with_.a.tangerine.png)

4. [Travels with a Tangerine](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/823863.Travels_with_a_Tangerine_A_Journey_in_the_Footnotes_of_Ibn_Battutah)  by Tim Mackintosh-Smith (October-December) | An engaging memoir of the  author’s experiences retracing the travels of Ibn Battuta, the greatest  traveller of the pre-mechanical age.

We’ll follow up with more details regarding these selections, and we’re open to suggestions as always.
